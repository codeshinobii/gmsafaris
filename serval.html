Unique Animal Encounters

Get Closer at Serval Wildlife

Experience a different kind of animal interaction at Serval Wildlife, located in the Siha District near Kilimanjaro. This unique facility offers the incredible opportunity to get up close and personal with a variety of African animals, many of which are habituated or rescued, in a controlled and safe environment.

Unlike traditional safaris focusing on viewing animals from a distance in vast parks, Serval Wildlife allows for guided, interactive encounters. Learn about fascinating species like servals, caracals, cheetahs, and monkeys from knowledgeable keepers, and capture unforgettable photographs. It's an excellent addition to a safari or Kilimanjaro itinerary, particularly for families and animal lovers seeking a more intimate experience.

Meet the Residents

Serval Wildlife provides a home for various animals, offering chances to learn about and interact with species such as:

Lions

Experience the power and majesty of the 'King of Beasts' in a safe environment.

Giraffes

Marvel at the towering height and unique patterns of these gentle giants.

Elands

See the largest species of antelope, known for their impressive size and gentle nature.

Wildebeests

Observe the unique 'gnu', famous for their role in the great migrations.

Zebras

Observe the iconic black and white stripes of these plains grazers up close.

Antelopes

Learn about and possibly see different types of antelope species resident at the facility.

What to Expect During Your Visit

  • Safety Briefing: Your visit begins with instructions on how to interact safely and respectfully with the animals.
  • Guided Tour: You'll be accompanied by knowledgeable keepers who guide you through the different animal enclosures or areas.
  • Information Sharing: Guides share interesting facts about each species, their conservation status, and individual animal stories (if applicable).
  • Interaction & Photos: Depending on the animal and safety guidelines, you may have opportunities for petting, feeding (supervised), or close-proximity photos.
  • Duration: Visits typically last a few hours, making it a manageable activity within a larger itinerary.

Best Time to Visit & Important Notes

Best Time to Visit
  • Year-Round Activity: Visits are generally possible throughout the year.
  • Dry Seasons (June - Oct & Jan - Feb): Offer more pleasant weather for outdoor interactions.
  • Mornings/Afternoons: Check recommended visiting times as animals may be more active during cooler parts of the day.
Important Considerations
  • Not a Wild Safari: Understand this is a controlled environment, not tracking animals in vast wilderness.
  • Booking Essential: Visits are usually by appointment only and need to be booked in advance.
  • Follow Rules: Adhere strictly to the guide's instructions for your safety and the animals' well-being.
  • Ethical Perspective: Consider the facility's focus (rescue, conservation, education) when planning your visit.
  • Age Restrictions: Check if there are minimum age limits for certain interactions.
